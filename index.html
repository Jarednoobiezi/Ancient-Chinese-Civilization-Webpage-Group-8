How did China Civilization Started?

The Ancient Chinese civilization started and developed at 2 impotant rivers/regions:
The Huang He or “Yellow” River to the North and the Yangtze River to the South.

These are very fertile regions however the land needs irrigation to make the crops grow,
and wellbuilt river embankments to prevent catastrophic flooding.
The river helps create fertile land suited for farming, during
certain times of the year.

The Chinese were able to advance independently of other world civilizations
thanks to this.

What are the Contributions of China?

The Ancient China Civilization is undoubtedly one the most innovative civilizations
and they are known for their technology and culture.

The Chinese civilization has made countless contributions to the world
in terms of physical things like:
- Paper making
- Gun powder
- Printing
- Silk
- Fireworks
and more...

The four great technologies of ancient China are:
papermaking, printing, gunpowder, and the compass were important contributions to global culture.
These was created for the first time in China.

The Ancient Chinese Dynasties

China is a sizable nation. Some dynasties sprang from various territories, while others merged their nearby regions into a single entity.
Additionally, dynasties contributed in many ways.

For instance, writing became more standardized,
ironworking became more advanced, and eminent philosophers like Confucius and Sun-Tzu lived
and taught throughout the Zhou Dynasty. The Terracotta Army was built by Qin Shi Huang
during the Qin Dynasty, and the Great Wall was repaired during the Ming Dynasty to defend China from Mongol invasions.

The dynasties that ruled China starting in its founding year of are listed below.
2070 B.C. to the 1912 resignation of its last emperor.

1. Xia Dynasty (c. 2070-1600 BC)

2. Shang Dynasty (c. 1600-1050 BC)

3. Zhou Dynasty (c. 1046-256 BC)

4. Qin Dynasty (221-206 BC)

5. Han Dynasty (206 BCE-220 AD)

6. Six Dynasties Period

7. Sui Dynasty (581-618)

8. Tang Dynasty (618-906)

9. Five Dynasties Period, Ten Kingdoms (907-960)

10. Song Dynasty (960-1279)

11. Yuan Dynasty (1279-1368)

12. Ming Dynasty (1368-1644)

13. Qing Dynasty (1644-1912)
